The Judicial Department

Federal Court System of the United States

The Judicial Department is one of the three branches of the United States Federal Government, responsible for interpreting the Constitution and federal laws. The federal court system consists of the Supreme Court and lower federal courts established by Congress.

Structure of the Federal Judiciary

Supreme Court of the United States:
The highest court in the land, consisting of the Chief Justice and Associate Justices. The Supreme Court has ultimate appellate jurisdiction over all federal and state court cases involving issues of federal law.

U.S. Courts of Appeals (Circuit Courts):
Thirteen appellate courts that hear appeals from the district courts within their circuits, as well as appeals from decisions of federal administrative agencies.

U.S. District Courts:
The trial courts of the federal court system. There are 94 federal judicial districts, including at least one district in each state, the District of Columbia, and Puerto Rico.
